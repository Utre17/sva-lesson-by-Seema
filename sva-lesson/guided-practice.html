üéµ Pronunciation Tips for Verb Endings

üîä Mastering the -S Sound

The -s ending in verbs has three different sounds. Practice saying these out loud:

/s/ sound: "works" "makes" "takes" "speaks"

/z/ sound: "goes" "plays" "runs" "learns"

/…™z/ sound: "teaches" "watches" "passes" "fixes"

Practice tip: Say each word slowly, then speed up naturally. The -s should flow smoothly with the rest of the word.

üí¨ Exercise 1: Complete the Conversations

Practice speaking these dialogues out loud. Choose the correct verb, then say the whole conversation:

üìû Scenario A: Calling a Restaurant

Receptionist: "Hello, how can I help you?"

You: "Hi, I'd like to make a reservation. My family to eat at your restaurant tonight."

In speech, "family" is treated as one unit, so use "wants". Practice saying: "My family wants to eat there."

üè¢ Scenario B: Team Meeting at Work

Manager: "How is the project going?"

You: "Everyone on our team working hard. We we'll finish on time."

"Everyone" is always singular in English speech. "We" is always plural. Practice the rhythm!

‚òï Scenario C: Coffee Shop Chat

Friend: "How are your kids doing in school?"

You: "Both of my children their new teachers. My son soccer after school."

"Both" signals plural, so "love". "My son" is one person, so "plays". Listen to the natural rhythm!

Try variations: "My wife needs..." "The kids want..." "We prefer..."

üí° Speaking Tips:
