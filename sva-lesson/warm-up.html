🏃‍♀️ Lesson 2: Warm-Up

Let's discover subject-verb agreement through a fun detective story!

🎯 Why This Matters

Subject-verb agreement is everywhere in writing! When subjects and verbs don't agree, sentences sound awkward and can confuse readers. Think of it like a dance - the subject and verb need to move together in harmony. Let's become grammar detectives and spot the mistakes!

📖 Detective Story: The Mystery of the Missing Homework

Your mission: Read this story and identify sentences where the subject and verb don't agree. Some sentences are correct!


Detective Sarah walk into the classroom on Monday morning. The students looks worried because their homework has disappeared! "The teacher want answers," whispers Jake. Sarah notices that the papers was scattered on the floor. Three pencils lies near the window. "Someone have been here," she thinks. The mystery needs solving, and the clues points to the janitor's closet.

🔍 Your Detective Work

Click on each underlined word to check if it agrees with its subject. Use the hints if you need help!

1. "Detective Sarah walk into the classroom..."
Sarah is one person (singular), so the verb needs to end in -s.
2. "The students looks worried..."
Students is plural (more than one), so the verb should NOT end in -s.
3. "their homework has disappeared!"
Homework is singular (even though it might seem like a lot of work!), so 'has' is correct.
4. "The teacher want answers..."
The teacher is one person (singular), so the verb needs -s.
5. "the papers was scattered..."
Papers is plural, so use 'were' instead of 'was'.

🎉 Great Detective Work!

Key Takeaway: Subject-verb agreement is like matching puzzle pieces. Singular subjects (one person/thing) pair with singular verbs, and plural subjects (more than one) pair with plural verbs. In most cases, singular verbs end in -s, while plural verbs don't!
