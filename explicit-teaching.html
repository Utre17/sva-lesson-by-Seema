üîç The Golden Rules

Rule #1: Basic Agreement

  • Singular subjects (one person/thing) ‚Üí singular verbs (usually with -s)
  • Plural subjects (more than one) ‚Üí plural verbs (usually no -s)

üìñ Example in Context: The School Play

Singular: The actress performs beautifully on stage.
Plural: The actors perform beautifully on stage.

Singular: Each student memorizes their lines carefully.
Plural: All students memorize their lines carefully.

Rule #2: Tricky Cases

üî∏ Collective Nouns (groups acting as one)

Story Context: "The team practices every day after school. The crowd cheers loudly when the players score. The family celebrates together after the game."

Even though these groups contain multiple people, they act as one unit, so they take singular verbs.

üî∏ Indefinite Pronouns

Always Singular: Someone knocks at the door. Everyone loves the new teacher. Nobody wants to miss the party.

Always Plural: Many believe in the cause. Few understand the complexity. Both agree on the solution.

üéØ Key Takeaways

  • Basic Rule: Singular subjects + singular verbs, Plural subjects + plural verbs
  • Memory Trick: In most cases, if the subject is singular, add -s to the verb
  • Special Cases: Collective nouns and indefinite pronouns like "everyone" are usually singular
  • Practice Makes Perfect: The more you practice, the more natural it becomes!
